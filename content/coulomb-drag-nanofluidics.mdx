At the Nick Holonyak Jr. Micro/Nano Lab, I worked on modeling **ion–electron Coulomb drag** effects in silicon nanochannels enhanced with **carbon nanotube (CNT) architectures**.

The goal: predict **open-circuit voltage** and **short-circuit current** in these nanofluidic systems and explore whether CNTs could improve momentum transfer.

## Why Coulomb Drag Matters

Recent work ([Nano Energy, 2023](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.nanoen.2023.108860)) showed that ionic flow can induce:

* **Electron drag** — ions pull electrons through momentum transfer
* **Charge separation** — creating measurable voltage
* **Measurable current** — enabling energy harvesting at the nanoscale

Improving this coupling leads to new **energy harvesting devices** and **low-power sensors**.

## The Physical Mechanism

When ions flow through nanochannels:

1. **Momentum transfer** occurs via Coulomb interactions
2. **Electrons get dragged** along with ionic flow
3. **Charge accumulation** creates voltage difference
4. **Current flows** if circuit is closed

The efficiency depends on:

* Channel geometry
* Ion concentration
* Surface properties
* Carrier mobility

## Simulation Approach

### 1. Solving the Poisson–Nernst–Planck (PNP) Equations

We modeled:

* **Ionic concentration gradients** — diffusion + drift
* **Electric potentials** — self-consistent field
* **Coupled boundary effects** — surface charge, EDL

```python
# Simplified PNP system
∇·(ε∇φ) = -ρ  # Poisson equation
∂n/∂t = -∇·J   # Continuity
J = -D∇n - μnE  # Nernst-Planck flux
```

### 2. Momentum Transfer Simulation

We computed drag via collision integrals:

```
F_drag = q_i * n_i * (E + μ_i * ∇φ)
```

Where:
* `q_i` — ionic charge
* `n_i` — ion concentration
* `μ_i` — ionic mobility
* `E` — electric field

### 3. CNT-Enhanced Architecture

CNTs improve performance by:

**Reducing surface scattering:**
* Smoother channel walls
* Less ion-surface friction
* Higher mobility

**Increasing charge carrier density:**
* CNTs provide additional conduction paths
* Enhanced electron availability
* Better coupling

**Improving momentum coupling:**
* Aligned CNT bundles guide flow
* Reduced boundary layer thickness
* More efficient energy transfer

## Simulation Setup

**Channel dimensions:**
* Length: 1-10 μm
* Width: 50-200 nm
* CNT diameter: 1-2 nm

**Material properties:**
* Silicon substrate (doped)
* Aligned CNT arrays (metallic)
* Ionic solution (KCl, NaCl)

**Boundary conditions:**
* Pressure-driven flow
* Fixed ion concentration at inlet/outlet
* Grounded electrodes

## Results

### Open-Circuit Voltage

CNT channels showed **12-18% higher voltage output** compared to bare silicon:

| Channel Type | Voltage (mV) | Improvement |
| ------------ | ------------ | ----------- |
| Bare Si      | 45 ± 5       | Baseline    |
| CNT-Si       | 52 ± 4       | +15.6%      |

### Short-Circuit Current

Current density improved due to:

* Higher electron mobility
* Better coupling efficiency
* Reduced back-scattering

### Efficiency Metrics

The key metric is **drag coefficient** (momentum transfer per ion):

```
η = (electron velocity) / (ion velocity)
```

CNT channels achieved **η ≈ 0.15-0.22** vs **η ≈ 0.08-0.12** for bare silicon.
